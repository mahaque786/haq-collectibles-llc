Service

Consignment

For high-value or specialized items, consignment may get you a better return than an outright sale. We handle the marketing, authentication, and transaction‚Äîyou get the proceeds minus our commission.

How Consignment Works:

Initial Review

We evaluate your item and determine if it's suitable for consignment.

Agreement & Transfer

We agree on terms, minimum price, and timeframe. You transfer the item to us.

Marketing & Sale

We photograph, list, and market your item to our network of buyers.

Payment

Once sold, you receive the proceeds minus our agreed commission.

Commission Structure:

Our commission rates are competitive and vary based on item value and category. Typical rates range from 15-25%. We'll discuss exact terms before entering any agreement.

Insurance & Security:

All consigned items are insured while in our possession. We maintain detailed records and photographs, and provide regular status updates.

Ideal for Consignment:

High-value individual pieces, rare items with specialized markets, collections that benefit from individual sale rather than bulk pricing, and items that may need time to find the right buyer.

Service

Procurement

Can't find what you're looking for? Let us source it for you. We leverage our network of dealers, auction houses, and private collectors to locate specific items on your behalf.

How It Works:

Tell Us What You Need

Describe the item, condition requirements, and budget range.

We Search

We tap our network and monitor markets for matching items.

Review Options

When we find candidates, we present them for your approval.

Acquisition

We handle the purchase, authentication, and delivery to you.

Fee Structure:

Our procurement fee is typically a percentage of the item's purchase price, usually 10-20% depending on complexity. For very rare items requiring extensive searching, we may discuss alternative arrangements. We'll always agree on terms before beginning a search.

Discretion & Privacy:

All procurement requests are handled with complete discretion. We understand that collectors often prefer to keep their acquisitions private, and we respect that fully.

What We Can Source:

Specific coins or currency, particular book editions, key trading cards, rare games, vintage electronics, and more. If it exists in the collectibles market, we can look for it.
